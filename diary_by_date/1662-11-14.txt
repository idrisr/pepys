1662-11-14

She begun to talk in the morning and to be friends, believing
all this while that I had read her letter, which I perceive by her
discourse was full of good counsel and relating the reason of her
desiring a Woman and how little charge she did entend it to be to
me. So I begun and argued it so full and plain to her, and she to
reason it highly to me to put her away and take one of the Bowyers
if I did dislike her, that I did resolve, when the house is ready, she
shall try her for a while.
