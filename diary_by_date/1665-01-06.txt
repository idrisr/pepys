1665-01-06

Lay long in bed, but most of it angry and scolding with my
wife about her warning Jane our cook-maid to be gone — and upon
that, she desires to go abroad today to look a place. A very good
maid she is and fully to my mind, being neat — only, they saya
little
459
apt to scold; but I hear her not. To my office all the morning, busy.
Dined at home. To my office again, being pretty well reconciled to
my wife; which I did desire to be, because she had designed much
mirth today to end Christmas with among her servants. At night
home, being Twelfenight, and there chose my piece of cake, but
went up to my vial and then to bed, leaving my wife and people up
at their sports, which they continue till morning, not coming to bed
at all.
