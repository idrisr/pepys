1662-12-23

At noon home to dinner with my wife alone. And after
dinner sat by the fire and then up to make up my accounts with
her, and find that my ordinary housekeeping comes to 7/ a month
— which is a great deal. By and by comes Dr. Pierce; who among
other things, tells me that my Lady Castlemaynes interest at Court
encreases and is more and greater then the Queenes. That she hath
brought in Sir H. Bennet and Sir Ch. Barkeley; but that the
Queene is a most good lady and takes all with the greatest
meekness that may be. He tells me too, that the King is much
concerned in the Chancellors sickness; and that the Chancellor is as
