1660-01-12

I drink my morning [draught] at Harpers with Mr. Sheply
and a seaman; and so to my office, where Capt. Holland came to see
me and appointed a meeting in the afternoon. Then I wrote letters
to Hinchingbrooke and sealed them at Wills, and after that went
home; and thence to the Half Moon, where I found the Captain and
Mr. Billingsly and Newman, a barber; where we were very merry
and had the young man that plays so well on the Welch harp.
Billingsly paid for all. Thence home, and finding my letters this day
