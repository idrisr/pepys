1662-04-11

Upearly to my lute and a song. Then about 6 a-clock with Sir
W. Pen by water to Deptford and among the ships now going to
Portugall with men and horse, to see them dispatched. So to
Greenwich; and had a fine pleasant walk to Woolwich, having in
our company Capt. Minnes, with whom, I was much pleased to
hear him talk, in fine language but pretty well for all that. Among
other things, he and the other Captains that were with us tell me

evening to the Exchange and spoke with Uncle Wight; and so home
and walked with my wife on the leads late; and so the barber came
to me; and so to bed very weary, which I seldom am.
