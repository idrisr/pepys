1665-06-10

Lay long in bed; and then up and at the office all the morning.
At noon dined at home, and then to the office, busy all the
afternoon. In the evening home to supper, and there to my great
trouble hear that the plague is come into the City (though it hath
these three or four weeks since its beginning been wholly out of the
City); but where should it begin but in my good friend and
neighbour’s, Dr. Burnett in Fanchurch street — which in both points
troubles me mightily.
