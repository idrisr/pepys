1667-05-06

Up; and angry with my maids for letting in watermen and I
know not who, anybody that they are acquainted with, into the
kitchen to talk and prate with them, which I will not endure. Then
out and by coach to my Lord Treasurer’s, who continues still very
ill. Then to Sir Ph. Warwicke’s house and there dida little business
about my Tanger tallies; and so to Westminster hall and there to the
Exchequer to consult about some way of getting our poor creditors
